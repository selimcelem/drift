You're a speck of light falling through the cosmos. Stars, planets, and black holes scroll past — but instead of dodging them with thrusters, you use their gravity. Pull yourself toward the nearest body. Push yourself away. Thread the needle. Don't get absorbed.

Two inputs. That's it.

LEFT — attract toward the nearest celestial body
RIGHT — repel away from the nearest celestial body

The physics only cares about the closest body to you at any given moment. A soft ring highlights which one is pulling the strings. A dashed line appears when you're actively attracting or repelling so you can read exactly what's about to happen.

Survive. Your score is the number of bodies you've drifted past. Speed ramps up as you go — after that, it's pure precision.

THREE DIFFICULTY MODES

NORMAL — Learn the gravity dance. Forgiving speed ramp, fewer bodies on screen.
HARD — Tighter spacing, faster ramp, more bodies. You need to read the field ahead.
EXTREME — Maximum density, aggressive speed. For players who've internalized the physics.

FOUR POWERUPS

SHIELD — Absorb one collision. The planet is destroyed, the shield shatters. Lasts 15 seconds if unused.
NOVA — Triple expanding shockwave. Three rings blast outward from your position, destroying everything they sweep past.
HYPERSPEED — Speed spikes to 3x. A glowing barrier plows ahead of you, destroying planets in your path. Gradually decelerates back to normal over 3 seconds.
GHOST — Phase through everything for 8 seconds. Bodies ripple as you clip through them.

Powerups participate in the gravity system — they're objects you can attract toward or repel from, just like planets. Navigate to them or accidentally avoid them.

ONLINE LEADERBOARD

Compete globally. Top 10 scores per difficulty, updated in real time. Pick a pilot name and see how you stack up. Your scores are submitted automatically on death.

WHAT THIS GAME IS NOT

No ads. No in-app purchases. No loot boxes. No energy system. No social login. No tracking. No notifications. No battle pass.

Just gravity, reflexes, and the void.

Built with vanilla HTML5 Canvas. One file. No frameworks. Runs at 60fps.
