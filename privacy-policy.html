Privacy Policy for Drift

Last updated: 10 April 2026

Drift is a free single-player mobile game developed by Selim Celem. This policy explains what data the app does and does not handle.

Data we collect

Drift collects the following data when you use the online leaderboard:

  • Username — a name you choose freely on first launch. This is not your real name, email, or any account identifier. You can change it at any time from the pause menu.
  • Score and difficulty — your score and the difficulty mode you were playing when the score was achieved.

That's it. Drift does not collect your real name, email address, device identifiers, IP addresses, usage analytics, advertising IDs, or telemetry of any kind. No account registration is required.

Data stored on your device

Drift stores the following locally on your device using the browser's localStorage API:

  • Your chosen username
  • Your top 5 local high scores
  • Your mute preference

This data never leaves your device except for the username and scores submitted to the online leaderboard as described below. Uninstalling the app or clearing its storage removes it permanently.

Online leaderboard

When you die in the game, your username, score, and difficulty are submitted to an online leaderboard. This data is:

  • Sent to an API hosted on Amazon Web Services (AWS) in the eu-west-1 (Ireland) region
  • Stored in AWS DynamoDB with a 90-day time-to-live (TTL), after which it is automatically deleted
  • Visible to other players on the global leaderboard (username and score only)

The leaderboard submission is fire-and-forget — if you are offline or the request fails, no data is sent and the game continues normally.

Third-party services

Drift does not integrate with any third-party analytics, advertising networks, or crash reporters. The only external services used are:

  • AWS API Gateway + Lambda + DynamoDB — for the online leaderboard, hosted in eu-west-1 (Ireland)
  • Google Fonts — a web font loaded on first launch if a connection is available; if offline, a system fallback is used

Permissions

Drift does not request any Android runtime permissions. It does not access your camera, microphone, location, contacts, storage, or any other sensitive APIs. An internet connection is used for the leaderboard but is not required to play.

Children's privacy

Drift is safe for all ages. The only data collected is a self-chosen username and game scores — no personal information, no real names, no contact details. The app is compliant with COPPA and similar regulations protecting children's privacy.
